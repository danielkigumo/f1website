About This Site

Why I built it and what F1 actually is.

Why I chose F1

I needed a topic for my web development project and F1 was the first thing that made sense. I watched it for a while — got into it mainly because of George Russell, who I thought was genuinely one of the more interesting drivers on the grid. Eventually I stopped watching because the races started feeling long and I did not always have the time, but I still think the sport itself is fascinating from a technical and competitive standpoint.

The project gave me a reason to actually look into it properly — the teams, the standings, how the points system works. This site covers the 2026 season with driver profiles, team information, and a news section.

What is Formula 1?

Formula 1 is the top level of single-seater car racing. The word "formula" refers to the rules every team has to follow when building their car — things like engine size, weight limits, and aerodynamic restrictions. Within those rules, the teams try to build the fastest car possible.

The season runs from March to November with around 24 races in different countries. At the end, whoever has the most points wins the World Championship — there is one for the drivers and a separate one for the constructors (the teams). It has been running since 1950.

What makes it interesting to me is the engineering side. These cars do 370 km/h, pit stops take under 3 seconds, and the difference between winning and losing is often less than a tenth of a second. There is a lot going on beyond just driving fast.

Quick Facts

The Speed

Top speed is around 370 km/h. Drivers pull up to 5G of force through fast corners, which is roughly five times their own body weight pushing against them.

The Pit Stops

A full tyre change takes under 3 seconds with a team of over 20 mechanics. The record is around 1.8 seconds. That part never gets old to watch.

The Season

24 races in 2026 across 5 continents. 10 teams, 20 drivers. Ferrari holds the most constructor championships with 16, though Mercedes dominated the hybrid era.
